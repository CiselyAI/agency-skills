# The Cisely agency model — primer

> The worldview behind Cisely, condensed for modeling. Read this so you can converse fluently about
> *why* each concept exists, not just how to create it.

## The premise

A business, stripped of everything, is an **exchange of value between agents**. Processes, org charts,
product catalogues — all consequences. The irreducible fact: someone acts, someone acts in return,
value moves.

From this, one hard truth: **a business cannot control what its stakeholders expect of it.**
Expectations belong to the one who holds them. What a business *can* do is exactly two things —
**choose whom to serve** (that's what strategy really is) and **design how it meets them** (the
touchpoints). You don't bend the people; you choose your people and design your meeting with them.

## The concepts

**Agent** — anything with the capacity to act: a person, an organization, a group (a buying
committee, a household), a software **system**, an **AI agent**. The model does not privilege humans;
value flows wherever action happens.

**Persona** — the *archetype* an agent instantiates (*the enterprise buyer*, *the compliance officer*,
*the billing system*), held **invariant**. You model the archetype once, never a roster of
individuals. The persona is the **bearer of goals** — goals belong to *who you are*. Its `nature` is
one of HUMAN / ORGANIZATION / GROUP / SYSTEM / AI_AGENT.

**Goal** — an **emotional deliverable**: the latent state a persona reaches for (to be secure, to
belong, to be proud, to be free of a worry). Not a task or a number. Its defining property is that it
is **latent** — never observed directly, only inferred through its proxies. A business cannot author
a stakeholder's goal; it can only **discover** it.

**Operating Context** — a coherent slice of the business through which a persona is viewed and within
which it has a distinct set of expectations (*onboarding*, *security*, *billing*, *support*). A lens.
Contexts are as often **internal** (security, compliance — how employees, systems, auditors meet the
business) as external. The context is what turns a persona into a stakeholder.

**Stakeholder** — a **persona × context**: who you are, met in a particular frame. It bears two things
the persona alone cannot: **expectations**, and **principal-hood**. One persona becomes many
stakeholders. Principal-hood is *whose goals it acts on*: **free-standing** (its own principal — a
customer, a regulator) or **delegated** (acts for a principal — an employee for the company). The
same human is delegated at work and free-standing as a consumer — which is why principal-hood lives on
the stakeholder, not the persona.

**Expectation** — the **atom of demand**, and the *only directly-measurable thing in the model*. A
goal is latent; an expectation is its outward, contextual **proxy** ("this tool doesn't get in my
way"). You can't instrument the pride of a finished home; you can instrument the tool. Expectations
live on a gradient of articulation — **latent → emerging → defined** — and the central work of a
business is **discovery: moving an expectation up that gradient**. They carry a **valence**: PAIN (a
fear of going unmet) or GAIN (a hope). Caution: satisfying every expectation is *never proof* the goal
was met — your set of expectations is a hypothesis about what proxies the goal.

**Belief** — the shared **root**. On the business side, belief roots a strategy; on the agent side,
belief is the ground a goal grows out of. Belief is also the **lens of discovery**: what you believe
determines what you can even *perceive* in your stakeholders. The strongest claim in the model: the
strongest businesses are the ones where **what the business believes and what its customers believe
are the same thing**. Conviction strength runs assumed → observed → validated.

## The response side (purpose & strategy)

**Mission** — the WHY the business exists (its purpose, just cause, vision, values). A singleton.
Everything on the response side hangs from it.

**Strategy** — the HOW. Underneath the jargon, two choices: **whom to serve** (which personas, in
which contexts) and **which of their expectations to commit to** — and, with equal deliberateness,
**which to decline**. Strategy is the operator that selects from the unbounded surface of all
expectations. *A declined expectation is a decision, not an omission* — most real roadmap arguments
are, underneath, arguments about which expectations to decline.

**Initiative** — the WHAT: a concrete bet that executes a strategy and builds the touchpoints meeting
committed expectations. In Cisely an initiative carries a **falsifiable hypothesis** — a claim the
business can be proven wrong about, with an explicit falsification criterion. A bet you cannot prove
wrong is not a hypothesis.

**Metric** — the measure. Metrics measure strategies, initiatives, and — most powerfully —
**expectations**, which is the only observable evidence you will ever get that a latent goal is being
served. Readings (`observations`) accumulate over time.

## The loop

> belief & mission root a strategy → strategy commits to expectations and spawns initiatives →
> initiatives build touchpoints → touchpoints meet expectations → metrics measure whether they were
> met → that evidence reshapes the strategy → and, slowly, refines the goal it was serving.

Demand and response, joined at the touchpoint, audited by the metric.

## What the model produces

Because the graph holds all of this, two artifacts fall out as **views**, never maintained by hand:

- **Jobs-to-be-Done.** A job story — *"When [situation], I want to [motivation], so I can [outcome]"* —
  is a walk through the graph: **Operating Context** (when) → **Expectation** (I want to) → **Goal**
  (so I can), executed by a **Persona**. Discovering a job is the same act as moving its expectation up
  the articulation gradient.
- **Value Proposition Canvas.** The *customer profile* is the demand side (goals → jobs; pain-valence
  expectations → pains; gain-valence → gains). The *value map* is the response side (touchpoints →
  products/services; initiatives → pain relievers / gain creators). The **fit** is the
  expectation-meeting relationship, evaluated.

## The payoff

Modeled this way, the enterprise stops being a pile of documents and becomes a **graph you can
traverse**. Humans share one description of who is served and why; disagreements become locatable
(point at the node). And an AI agent dropped into the business discovers context by **traversal**
rather than being briefed — following edges from a touchpoint to the expectation it meets, to the
stakeholder, to the persona and its goal, to the belief, to the strategy that responds.
