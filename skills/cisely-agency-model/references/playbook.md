# Modeling playbook — interview & ingest

> How to actually elicit or extract the model. Use with `tool-map.md` (the build order) and
> `agency-model.md` (the worldview). Always **propose → confirm → write**.

## Guided interview

Don't march through a form. Follow the energy, but cover the model. Good openers and the concept each
discovers:

### Start with the WHY (Mission)
- "Why does this company exist — beyond making money? What would be lost from the world if it
  vanished?" → **Mission** purpose / just cause.
- "What do you believe about your market or your customers that others don't?" → seeds a **Belief**
  (capture these whenever they surface, all session long).

### The agents you serve (Personas)
- "Who are the kinds of parties you deal with? Don't name individuals — name the *types*." → **Personas**.
  Probe beyond customers: partners, regulators, internal teams, and the **systems and AI agents** that
  act in your business (each is an agent).
- For each: "Is this a person, an organization, a group, a software system, or an AI agent?" →
  `nature`.

### What they're really reaching for (Goals)
- "Forget features for a second. What is this persona ultimately trying to *feel* or *be*? Pride?
  Safety? Belonging? Control?" → **Goal** (an emotional deliverable, not a task).
- If they answer with a task ("they want a report"), push: "And if they had that, what does it let them
  *be*?" Keep going until you reach the latent emotional state.

### The frames you meet them in (Operating Contexts)
- "Where and how do you encounter this persona — onboarding, support, billing, compliance? Each is a
  different lens." → **Operating Contexts**. Remember internal contexts count.
- "Through this lens, what does the same persona want that it *doesn't* want through another lens?" →
  prepares the stakeholder split.

### The situated demand (Stakeholders → Expectations)
- Situate: "So *this persona* in *this context* — is it acting for itself (free-standing) or on behalf
  of someone else (delegated)?" → **Stakeholder** principal-hood.
- "Concretely, what does it expect *here*? What would make it feel the tool 'just works'? What does it
  fear going wrong?" → **Expectations** (gain-valence hopes, pain-valence fears).
- "How clearly can they even state this — is it a crisp requirement, or a vague unease?" → articulation
  (latent → emerging → defined). Naming the vague ones *is* the discovery.
- Link each expectation to the goal it proxies; ask "is this really a stand-in for [the goal we named]?"

### How you choose to win (Strategy → Initiatives → Metrics)
- "Of everyone you *could* serve and everything they *could* expect — who and what do you commit to?
  And, just as important, what do you deliberately **decline**?" → **Strategy**.
- "What's the bet you're making to deliver this? What would prove that bet **wrong**?" → **Initiative**
  + its falsifiable hypothesis.
- "How would you *know* it's working — what would you measure?" → **Metric** (prefer one that measures
  an expectation).

### Close on alignment
- "Does the conviction behind this strategy match a belief your customers already hold?" → root the
  strategy and a goal in the **same Belief** where they genuinely coincide.

## Ingest from material

When the user points you at decks, docs, transcripts, a website, or a codebase:

1. **Read widely, extract candidates.** Pull out: parties mentioned (→ personas, with a guessed
   nature), what they seem to want (→ goals/expectations), the situations they appear in (→ contexts),
   stated convictions (→ beliefs), and any strategy / initiatives / metrics language.
2. **Classify, don't just collect.** For each candidate, decide which concept it is and flag
   uncertainty. A feature is *not* a goal — trace it to the emotional deliverable it serves. A KPI is a
   metric; what expectation does it proxy?
3. **Present the proposed graph** as a structured outline (personas with their goals; contexts;
   stakeholders; expectations and the goals they proxy; mission/strategy/initiatives/metrics) **before
   writing anything**. Mark what's confident vs. inferred.
4. **Confirm and correct** with the user, then write in dependency order (see `tool-map.md`).
5. **Name the gaps.** Tell them what the material *didn't* reveal — usually the latent goals and the
   declined expectations. That gap is the next discovery.

## Either way

- Keep a running list of **open questions** and **assumptions** to revisit; the model is never
  finished, it's continuously re-checked.
- Prefer fewer, well-activated nodes over many hollow drafts.
- After each write, surface the **View in Cisely** link so the user sees it land.
